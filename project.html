Introduction to AMBA Bus Architecture

AMBA(Advanced Microcontroller Bus Architecture) in developed by ARM Ltd. to standardize on-chip communication.

Types of AMBA buses:

1. AHB(Advanced High-performance Bus) --> High-speed
2. AXI(Advanced extensible Interface) --> very High-speed
3. APB(Advanced peripheral Bus) -->Low-power, low complexity

Disadvantages:

• Complexity.
• Limited Features.
• Limited Scalability.

Advantages:

• Simple and cost-effective.
• Supports multiple peripherals.
• Low power consumption.
• Easy to expand.

Applications:

• Microcontroller.
• SoC(System on chip).
• FPGA- based embedded systems.
• Peripheral interfacing(UART, SPI, 12C).

Conclusion:

The AMBA APB bridge plays a crucial role in:
• Bridging high-speed buses(AHB/AXI) with peripherals.
• Managing communication efficiently.
